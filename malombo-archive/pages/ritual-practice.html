Ritual Practice

Possession, healing, drumming, and trance

Venda Malombo ritual treats sound as the animating core of healing: an embodied bridge between the afflicted person, the community, and ancestral agency.

How ritual practice works

Malombo ceremonies are initiated when illness or disturbance is interpreted through lineage and spirit relations. Music is not background. It structures attention, motion, call-and-response, and the conditions of possession itself [5].

  • Possession: the afflicted person is drawn into heightened bodily receptivity.
  • Healing: ritual sound helps diagnose imbalance and restore relation with ancestors.
  • Drumming: cyclical rhythm sustains continuity and intensification.
  • Trance: repetition, chant, and communal motion cultivate altered states without severing social meaning.
