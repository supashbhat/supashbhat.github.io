Read It

What the numbers mean

Barnes-Hut is not “faster no matter what.” It introduces tree-building and traversal overhead, which means the exact method can still win at smaller `N`. The point is to locate the crossover where approximation begins paying off, then improve the implementation and visualization from there.

Smaller `theta` values favor accuracy. Larger `theta` values favor speed. The useful engineering question is not which one is “right,” but what tradeoff remains legible and acceptable for a given simulation goal.
